Why Sell Mineral Rights?

  • The Risk
    You prefer to let the buyer assume risk because you understand that things can go wrong with oil or gas wells or even an entire area of an oil or gas "play."
  • The Offer
    It's too good to pass up.
  • The Money
    You could use some extra cash.
  • The Time
    You are up in years and prefer money now rather than the possibility of it being many years away.
  • Hedging
    You prefer to sell a portion of your mineral rights and retain the rest, so that with your money from the sale, you are assured of at least some significant cash regardless of how it turns out for the oil or gas companies in the area.

Risks Associated with
Self Management

  • Equipment Failure
    Mechanical failure of the wellbore, such as a casing leak, forcing abandonment of the well.
  • Production Issues
    Salt water encroaches into the wellbore, killing the flow of oil or gas (saltwater is a byproduct of oil and gas production).
  • Lack of Production
    Oil or gas production does not last nearly as long as the oil company expected (oil and gas production is a very inexact science).
  • Oil Prices
    The price of oil or gas declines a lot, making the production unprofitable to continue.
  • Well Problems
    The production of oil or gas is too troublesome and problematic and the company elects to give up.
