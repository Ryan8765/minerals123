Selling Mineral Rights
3 Simple Steps

Step 1
Furnish your info

We need to verify your ownership of the mineral rights or royalty rights. Send us a copy of documents that show what you own. This can include check stubs, letters from an oil company, mineral deeds, probate documents, oil & gas leases, pooling agreements, plats or maps.

Step 2
Agree to mineral valuation

We have state-of-the-art oil & gas valuation software to do an assessment of your mineral rights value and determine the lump sum value. The common question of "What is the value of my mineral rights" will be answered. With your approval, we move forward to close your sale of mineral rights.

Step 3
Sign papers we prepare

At no cost to you, we verify your title and prepare all closing documents for your sale of mineral rights. Sign them before a notary and send back to us. We will pay for recording them in your county courthouse and send you your check. Enjoy the lump sum cash settlement!

The process to sell mineral rights is essentially the same whether you wish to sell oil royalties or sell gas royalties. And for some owners, there is no production. (There are no producing oil wells or gas wells.) In this case, just provide copies of your title documents to help us verify your ownership.

Why Sell Mineral Rights?

  • The Risk
    You prefer to let the buyer assume risk because you understand that things can go wrong with oil or gas wells or even an entire area of an oil or gas "play."
  • The Offer
    It's too good to pass up.
  • The Money
    You could use some extra cash.
  • The Time
    You are up in years and prefer money now rather than the possibility of it being many years away.
  • Hedging
    You prefer to sell a portion of your mineral rights and retain the rest, so that with your money from the sale, you are assured of at least some significant cash regardless of how it turns out for the oil or gas companies in the area.

Risks Associated with
Self Management

  • Equipment Failure
    Mechanical failure of the wellbore, such as a casing leak, forcing abandonment of the well.
  • Production Issues
    Salt water encroaches into the wellbore, killing the flow of oil or gas (saltwater is a byproduct of oil and gas production). Another possibility is unanticipated closure of the frack fissures.
  • Depletion
    Oil or gas production does not last nearly as long as the oil company expected (oil and gas production is a very inexact science).
  • Oil & Gas Prices
    The price of oil or gas declines a lot, making the production unprofitable to continue.

Why We Buy Mineral Rights

The oil business is one of the world's riskiest industries, but, obviously, it can be profitable. We are an experienced mineral buyer and our company is able to make a profit because we make lots of purchases, adding regularly to our portfolio that is scattered across thousands of acres across many states. If any one deal "goes bad" and is not profitable, it won't break us, as other deals fare better. The oil biz is very cyclical -- boom and bust. Of course, during a bust, we don't do so well but during a boom, profit increases and we can make up for hard times. Our "buy and hold" strategy works because we are in it for the long haul.

An individual does not have the luxury of spreading risk. Plus, people just need cash money for various things, or they don't want to wait it out for the mineral interests to produce income. To some, a mineral property finally producing income ten to twenty years down the road (or longer) is not appealing. And some mineral owners just get tired of dealing with it all; it can be very confusing and labor-intensive to manage mineral rights.
